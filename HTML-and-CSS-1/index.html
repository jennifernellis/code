Introducing..."Know Your Work"

Companies and corporations are very good at hiding their scandals and silencing employees. The chance of unknowingly working for a company with a history of discrimination on multiple accounts is very likely. It is also likely to fall prey to being a victim of discrimination in your workplace when you least expect it. There are steps you can take to avoid these scenarios, but it is not guaranteed that these steps will be effective. But what if there was a way to know for sure that you are entering a morally sound work space?

It is no debate of the many disappointing instances of discrimination in the work force. Many employees around the world have been discriminated against regarding things like race, sexuality, gender, religion, etc. Those employees probably did not imagine themselves being victims of discrimination while applying to their jobs. It’s often very hard to find history of a company’s poor treatment of its employees. Companies silence current employees from speaking out, and former-employees’ experiences do not reach nearly enough people for future applicants to know about.

So, what if if there was a way to know a company’s history before applying? With the new application/website “Know Your Work” you can know just exactly what you could be signing up for. Using keywords about the specific company from across a range of social media platforms (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, TikTok, etc.), we can provide you with current/past employees experiences in the field you are searching, so you can decide whether you think it’s the right environment for you. There is also a function on the website in which you can submit your own experience with a certain company directly to the website, which will also be used in the data results. Our homepage will provide you with recommended companies that have a solid track record of displaying no form of discrimination towards their workers. There will also be a page of companies you should probably steer clear from. You can personalize your experience on the website by entering things about yourself like race, gender, sexuality, etc. to your profile. You can also search other people’s profiles and message them any questions you may have about their experience working for a certain company. The whole point of this site is to use community to help each other be safe and avoid possible discriminatory corporations.
